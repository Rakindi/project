A fracture refers to a break or crack in a bone, and there are several types, each with distinct characteristics. An avulsion fracture occurs when a tendon or ligament pulls off a fragment of bone. A comminuted fracture involves the bone breaking into multiple pieces, often due to high-impact trauma. In a fracture dislocation, the bone is broken and displaced, affecting the joint’s alignment. A greenstick fracture is an incomplete fracture common in children, where the bone bends and cracks but doesn't break all the way through. A hairline fracture is a fine, thin crack, often not easily visible on an X-ray. In an impacted fracture, the bone fragments are driven into each other, typically from a fall or collision. A longitudinal fracture runs parallel to the length of the bone, while an oblique fracture occurs at an angle across the bone. Pathological fractures happen due to weakened bones, often from conditions like osteoporosis or cancer.

Finally, a spiral fracture is caused by a twisting or rotational force, resulting in a helical break pattern. Each type of fracture requires specific treatment depending on severity, location, and the patient’s health condition. Proper diagnosis and intervention are crucial for optimal healing.
